THE

QUATERNARY PERIOD

The latest geologic period.

Quick facts:

- The Quaternary period is the most recent geological time period

- The Quaternary period is about 2.6 million years old

The Quaternary Period is the geological period we are living in today. During the Quaternary Period, humans evolved and distributed themselves all over the world. Other species have adapted to the changes as well. A series of glacial advances and retreats bring about the extinction and variation of species. Whales and sharks emerge and dominate the sea, while mammals with thick hair roam the land, feeding on any plants that might grow in the frigid climate caused by the ice.

History:

The Quaternary Period was named for following terms such as "Primary" and "Secondary". In 1948, it was recognized by the International Geological Congress as an official geological period. It is composed of two epochs: the Holocene (recent) and Pleistocene (older). The Holocene has seen human growth and also the impact of it on the world's ecosystems. The Quaternary Period overall has been very well-documented, for obvious reasons.

Quick facts:

- The Quaternary period is the most recent geological time period (ESRT pg. 8)

- The Quaternary period started 1.8 million years old (ESRT pg. 8)

- Fossils from the Quaternary can be found in New York State on Long Island as sedimentary rock from this period has been deposited here by the last glacial advance/retreat (ESRT pg. 8)

- Some examples of species that lived during the Quaternary are: Beluga whales, Mastodonts, and Condors (ESRT pg. 9)

- The advance and retreat of the last continental glacier affected New York State by creating Long Island during the Quaternary (ESRT pg. 9)

About:

The Quaternary Period is the geological period we are living in today. During the Quaternary Period, humans evolved and distributed themselves all over the world. Other species have adapted to the changes as well. A series of glacial advances and retreats bring about the extinction and variation of species. Whales and sharks emerge and dominate the sea, while mammals with thick hair (sometimes called megafauna) roam the land, feeding on any plants that might grow in the frigid climate caused by the ice.

Above: Megafauna such as the wooly mammoth roam the Earth.

History:

The Quaternary Period was named for following terms such as "Primary" and "Secondary". In 1948, it was recognized by the International Geological Congress as an official geological period. It is composed of two epochs: the Holocene (recent) and Pleistocene (older). The Holocene has seen human growth and also the impact of it on the world's ecosystems. The Quaternary Period overall has been very well-documented, for obvious reasons.

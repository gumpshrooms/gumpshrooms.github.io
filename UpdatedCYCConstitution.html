The Chinese Youth Club of Western New York

Constitution

  1. OBJECTIVES OF ORGANIZATION
  1.  To heighten the awareness of Chinese culture in the children of the community.
  2. To aid the Chinese Club of Western New York in its events and functions, primarily those involving child members.
  3. To promote and perpetuate fellowship among members of the organization and to cultivate a spirit of cooperation.
  1. MEMBERSHIP
  1. Membership is open to all persons grades 1 through 12 who either
  1. are of Chinese descent.
  2. have an active interest in China, Taiwan and/or Chinese culture.
  1. All members must have a parent who is a member of the Chinese Club of Western New York.
  2. Duties and Privileges of the Members:
  1. All members must fulfill all obligations of the Club.
  2. All members shall have the right to elect officers for the organization.
  3. All members shall have the privilege of participating in all of the social activities and meetings.
  1. ADVISORS
  1. Adult advisor
  1. will be the Vice President of the Chinese Club of Western New York.
  2. will supervise all activities and meetings of this organization.
  3. will provide advice and suggestions to the officers.
  4. will act as a representative of the organization at meetings of the Chinese Club of Western New York.
  5.  will apply and provide funding for the Youth Club from the Chinese Club of Western New York.
  1. the annual allocated sum.
  1. Officers are required to present a proposal for the allocated sum to the President of the Chinese Club of Western New York.
  2. President of the Chinese Club of Western New York will present the proposal to the board members of the Chinese Club of Western New York, who will delegate and present the Chinese Youth Club of Western New York with the appropriate allocated sum.
  1. any additional funds deemed necessary and proper for worthwhile activities.
  1. Senior advisor
  1. will be the president of the past year.
  2. will act as a mentor to the current officers.
  3. will aid the adult advisor in supervising all activities and meetings of this organization.
  1. MEETING PROCEDURES
  1. The lunar year of the organization is from February 15th through February 14th of the next calendar year.
  2. Officers are required to conduct at least one general meeting or activity every 3 months for all members of the organization.
  3. The Secretary will keep a record of attendance for all meetings and events.
  4. Members will be required to attend at least two meetings or activities per year unless a valid reason is provided to the officers.
  1. OFFICERS
  1. Application/Nomination
  1. At the end of the lunar year, current officers will inform the members about the upcoming election, in writing, two weeks in advance.
  2. Applicants for President, Vice President, Secretary, and Treasurer must
  1. complete a written application.
  2. for President: be in high school and have at least one (1) full year of leadership experience with any organization. Preference will be given to prior CYC officers. The candidate must be able to prove his/her’s term as an officer to be eligible for candidacy.
  3. for Vice President, Secretary, Treasurer, Program Director: be in grades 8 through 11 at the time of the election.
  4. for President, Vice President, Secretary, Treasurer, Program Director: if there are more than 4 applicants for an office, only 4 will be approved as candidates to run in the election.
  1. Applicants for Assistant Secretary, Assistant Treasurer, and Assistant Program Director
  1. must be in grades 8 through 11 at the time of the election.
  2. there can be a maximum of 9 candidates for the 3 positions.
  1. An applicant must have his application endorsed by the current officers before being considered a candidate.
  2. An applicant can apply for no more than two offices per year.
  1. Election
  1. All members in grades 5 through 12 must be allowed to vote for every office. The adult advisor is not included in the voting process.
  2. The election will be a secret ballot tallied by the current officers and the advisors.
  3. Candidates must be prepared to answer questions posed by the panel at the election.
  1. The panel will consist of the current President, the senior advisor, the adult advisor, and any other officers, excluding the Assistants, not running for office.
  2. All candidates will be asked the same number of unknown questions.
  3. The unknown question will be selected by the panel.
  1. If any member has reason to suspect that the election process was tainted by blackmail, bribery, deceit, or any unethical practices, the officers and advisors have the right to take necessary measures to resolve the issue.
  2. The candidate receiving the most votes will become the new officer. In the case of a tie, there will be a re-vote for only those candidates involved in the tie. In the case of another tie, there will be a second re-vote. In the case of yet another tie, the attending officers will vote amongst themselves to decide the winner.
  3. In event there are no candidates for the Assistant position, the candidate receiving the second most votes when running for Secretary, Treasurer, or Program Director will automatically be declared to the Assistant position.
  1. Responsibilities
  1. President
  1. To determine, conduct, and call meetings.
  2. To act as a representative of the organization at meetings of the Chinese Club of Western New York.
  3. To correspond with the officer board of the Chinese Club of Western New York.
  1. Vice President
  1. To act as a substitute for the President in event of illness or absence.
  2. To assist the President at all events and meetings
  1. Secretary
  1. To record minutes of the meetings.
  2. To keep a record of attendance for all events.
  3. To conduct all correspondences of the organization and maintain the Chinese Youth Club website.
  1. Treasurer
  1. To handle all financial matters of the organization.
  2. To issue a financial statement at each meeting.
  3. To maintain the Chinese Youth Club of Western New York Treasury Book. All receipts must be kept.
  1. Program Director
  1. To handle all Chinese Youth Club activity correspondences.
  2. To handle planning for the major social events of the lunar year. [Easter Egg Hunt, Mother’s Day Celebration, Chinese New Year Performance, etc.]
  1. Assistant Secretary
  1. To act as a substitute for the Secretary in event of illness or absence.
  2. To assist the Secretary at all events and meetings.
  1. Assistant Treasurer
  1. To act as a substitute for the Treasurer in event of illness or absence.
  2. To assist the Treasurer at all events and meetings.
  1. Assistant Program Director
  1. To act as a substitute for the Program Director in event of illness or absence.
  2. To assist the Program Director at all events and meetings.
  1. All officers, once accepting their election or appointment, will be obliged to fulfill all responsibilities of their position. They cannot resign unless in extenuating circumstances, and an adequate reason must be given to and reviewed by the other officers.
  2. Officers must hold monthly meetings or engage in monthly correspondence.
  3. All officers must be present at all meetings unless a valid reason is provided to an attending officer.
  1. EXPULSION & DISCIPLINE
  1. Officers and/or advisors reserve the right to discipline or expel a member who does not follow the procedures and regulations of the organization and the community.
  2. Officers can be removed from office if they
  1. do not fulfill their responsibilities.
  2. are repeatedly absent from major events and activities.
  3. engage in unethical practices or improper behavior.
  1. Expulsion will be determined by consensus of the President, Vice President, Secretary, Treasurer, adult advisor, and senior advisor. However, if the member in question is one of those in the preceding list, they will be excluded from the process.
  1. AMENDMENTS
  1. The proposal of new amendments must be submitted to the officers of the organization two weeks before each general meeting.
  2. Adoption of new amendments to the Constitution requires a 2/3-majority vote of the attending members.
  3. Ratification of the Constitution requires a 2/3-majority vote of the officers.
